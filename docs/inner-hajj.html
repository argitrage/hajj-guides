Imam Zayn al-Abidin (a) and al-Shibli

Imam Sajjad's Inner Hajj

A stage-by-stage spiritual checklist from the al-Islam.org translation of Imam Zayn al-Abidin's (a) discourse on Hajj with al-Shibli. This is for inner meaning and reflection, not legal rulings.

Spiritual section

Source

Based on The Spiritual Aspects of Hajj, A Translation of Imam Zayn al-Abidin's (a) Discourse on Hajj with al-Shibli on al-Islam.org, translated by Mohammed Ali Ismail. The article itself notes that this tradition is used for spiritual benefit and is not presented as a source of halal/haram rulings.

Ihram Makkah Safa & Marwa Hajj Days Return

Ihram and Miqat

Before the outward journey begins, the Imam (a) teaches al-Shibli to begin with the inward stripping away.

Miqat - Arrive with a new identity

  • Do this: intend to remove the clothing of disobedience and wear the clothing of obedience.
  • Ask: what old self am I refusing to carry into Hajj?

Removing sewn clothes - Remove hypocrisy

  • Do this: intend to remove showmanship, hypocrisy, and mistaken beliefs.
  • Ask: where do I act religious for people instead of Allah?

Ghusl - Wash sins away

  • Do this: intend to wash off mistakes and sins, not only dust and sweat.
  • Ask: which sin do I want to leave under this water?

Putting on Ihram - Sincere repentance

  • Do this: intend to be cleansed by the light of sincere repentance.
  • Ask: am I only feeling regret, or am I changing direction?

Ihram restrictions - Forbid disobedience

  • Do this: intend to forbid for yourself everything Allah has forbidden.
  • Ask: which forbidden habit have I normalized?

Knot of Hajj - Untie every knot not for Allah

  • Do this: intend to untie every attachment, contract, habit, and loyalty that is not for Allah.
  • Ask: what controls me when Allah's pleasure should control me?

Two rak'ahs of Ihram - Return to Salah

  • Do this: intend closeness to Allah through salah, the best action.
  • Ask: will my prayer after Hajj be different from before Hajj?

Talbiyah - Perfect obedience

  • Do this: say Labbayk as perfect obedience and a refusal of disobedience.
  • Ask: when I say "Here I am", what am I still holding back?

Makkah and the House

Seeing the sacred place should change the tongue, the heart, and the hand.

Entering the Haram - Stop backbiting

  • Do this: intend to forbid yourself from backbiting any Muslim.
  • Ask: whose honor have I treated lightly?

Reaching Makkah - Make Allah the purpose

  • Do this: intend in your heart that your purpose is Allah.
  • Ask: am I seeking Allah, or only the experience of Hajj?

Seeing the Ka'ba - See the direction of life

  • Do this: let the qiblah outside remind you of the qiblah inside.
  • Ask: do my choices face Allah as clearly as my body faces the Ka'ba?

Tawaf - Escape to Allah

  • Do this: intend that your circling is an escape to Allah, under His knowledge of your unseen self.
  • Ask: what am I running from, and what am I running toward?

Touching the corners - Guard the covenant

  • Do this: treat contact with the House as a serious covenant, not a crowd achievement.
  • Ask: will my hands after Hajj touch haram, harm, or humiliation?

Hajar al-Aswad - Allegiance to Allah

  • Do this: intend that giving your hand to the Black Stone is giving your hand to Allah.
  • Ask: am I invalidating this pledge through disobedience or unlawful gain?

Maqam Ibrahim - Stand for obedience

  • Do this: intend to stand for every act of obedience and turn your back on every act of disobedience.
  • Ask: where do I need Ibrahim-like firmness?

Prayer at Maqam - Humble Shaytan

  • Do this: intend the prayer of Ibrahim (a), and that your prayer humiliates Shaytan.
  • Ask: does my salah weaken Shaytan's hold over me?

Zamzam - Look to obedience

  • Do this: intend to look toward obedience and disregard disobedience.
  • Ask: what should this water heal in my religious life?

Safa and Marwa

Sa'y becomes the movement of a servant between fear and hope.

Sa'y - Escape to Allah

  • Do this: intend that your movement is fleeing to Allah, while He knows your hidden state.
  • Ask: what false refuge do I keep returning to?

Safa and Marwa - Hope and fear

  • Do this: intend that you are standing between hope in Allah's mercy and fear of your own accountability.
  • Ask: am I balanced, or do I use hope to avoid repentance and fear to avoid trust?

Hajj Days

Carry these intentions through Mina, Arafat, Muzdalifah, Mash'ar, Jamarat, sacrifice, and Masjid al-Khif.

Mina - Keep people safe from you

  • Do this: intend that people are safe from your tongue, heart, and hands.
  • Ask: who is unsafe around my speech, temper, suspicion, or actions?

Arafat - Recognize Allah's knowledge

  • Do this: remember that Allah knows every affair, your Book of Deeds, your inner self, and your heart.
  • Ask: what would I confess if I truly believed my record was open before Him?

Mountain of Mercy - Mercy for believers

  • Do this: intend that Allah is merciful to every believing man and woman and takes care of every Muslim.
  • Ask: can I ask mercy for others without narrowing Allah's mercy to myself?

Namirah - Command yourself first

  • Do this: intend not to command others before commanding yourself, and not to forbid others before forbidding yourself.
  • Ask: where am I strict with others and soft with myself?

Arafat signs - Witnesses for obedience

  • Do this: intend that these places witness your obedience and become protection for you by Allah's command.
  • Ask: what do I want this place to testify about me?

Muzdalifah - Prayer of thanks

  • Do this: intend gratitude on the eve of the tenth, asking Allah to ward off difficulty and open ease.
  • Ask: which hardship became a doorway to Allah for me?

Between the two signs - Do not swerve

  • Do this: intend not to swerve right or left from the true religion, neither by heart, tongue, nor limbs.
  • Ask: where do I bend religion to fit myself?

Picking stones - Remove ignorance

  • Do this: intend to remove disobedience and ignorance, and to gain beneficial knowledge and good deeds.
  • Ask: which ignorance am I still protecting because it excuses me?

Mash'ar al-Haram - Realize taqwa

  • Do this: intend that your heart realizes the realization of the people of piety and fear of Allah.
  • Ask: what would taqwa look like tomorrow morning?

Jamarat - Anger Iblis

  • Do this: intend to throw at your enemy Iblis and anger him by completing your Hajj.
  • Ask: which of Shaytan's invitations am I rejecting with this pebble?

Shaving / Taqsir - Leave sins behind

  • Do this: intend purification from the dirt and consequences of transgression, leaving sins behind like a newborn.
  • Ask: who was harmed by my old self, and what repair is now due?

Sacrifice - Cut the throat of greed

  • Do this: intend to cut the throat of greed through piety and follow the tradition of Ibrahim (a).
  • Ask: what am I sacrificing besides money?

Masjid al-Khif - Fear only Allah

  • Do this: intend not to fear anyone except Allah and your sins, and not to hope except in Allah's mercy.
  • Ask: whose opinion has become bigger than Allah in my heart?

Return to Makkah

The final tawaf should not be only an ending; it should be a return to obedience.

Final Tawaf - Return from mercy into obedience

  • Do this: intend to proceed from Allah's mercy, return to His obedience, hold to His love, perform His obligations, and seek nearness to Him.
  • Ask: what exact obligation, relationship, or habit will show that my Hajj came home with me?
  • Keep this as your last Hajj question: did I only perform the outward journey, or did I learn the inward one?
