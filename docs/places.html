Daily Spiritual Flow

Use this as the rhythm for each day, then add the place-specific section below.

Before Ihram and on the Way

  • Begin with tawbah: return anything owed, ask forgiveness from people, and renew the intention that every step is only qurbatan ilallah.
  • Before ihram, the recommended ghusl and the ihram duas are listed in the Sistani/al-Islam and duas.org Hajj dua pages.
  • Repeat talbiyah with presence, especially after prayers, waking, riding, dismounting, ascending, descending, and meeting other pilgrims.
  • Keep a private list of people who asked for duas; mention them by name at the Ka'ba, Arafat, Mash'ar, Mina, and near the Prophet (s).

Every Day, Wherever You Are

  • Start the day with intention: "Today I want Allah, not comfort, status, photos, or winning arguments."
  • After each wajib salah: tasbih of Sayyidah Fatimah (a), salawat, a short dua for Imam al-Mahdi (a), and one named person from your dua list.
  • Keep a Qur'an minimum: even a few verses with translation. In Makkah, Makarem lists reciting Qur'an and completing a recitation if possible among recommended acts.
  • When waiting: qadha salah of life, istighfar for a named sin, salawat, or silent service to another pilgrim.
  • End the day with muhasabah: one sin to repent from, one blessing to thank Allah for, one person to forgive, and one concrete change after Hajj.

8 Dhu al-Hijjah: Leaving Makkah for Mina

  • After ihram for Hajj, keep talbiyah alive as you leave Makkah. When heading toward Mina, ask Allah to fulfill your hopes and repair your deeds.
  • On reaching Mina, thank Allah for bringing you there safely, then ask Him to give you the same grace He gave His prophets and obedient servants.
  • If possible, spend the night in worship and prayer, especially in Masjid al-Khaif; keep the heart quiet and avoid empty talk.
  • Prepare your Arafat dua list before sleeping: tawbah, parents, spouse/family, teachers, the oppressed, the deceased, and the reappearance of Imam al-Mahdi (a).

9 Dhu al-Hijjah: Arafat

  • Make the day a day of confession, gratitude, and asking. Sistani/Makarem mention purity, ghusl near noon if possible, early Zuhr/Asr, and turning the heart fully toward Allah.
  • Read Dua Arafah of Imam Husayn (a), even if slowly in translation.
  • Repeat: istighfar, salawat, Surah al-Tawhid, takbir, and personal duas. Ask specifically for a Hajj that changes your character.
  • Leave Arafat with humility, not as someone finished, but as someone hoping to be forgiven.

Night of 10 Dhu al-Hijjah: Mash'ar / Muzdalifah

  • Walk or travel calmly from Arafat, asking forgiveness. Avoid rushing, pushing, or harming anyone.
  • Pray Maghrib and Isha there if possible according to your group's timing and rulings, then fill the night with dhikr, salawat, Qur'an, and rest for worship.
  • Collect pebbles with the thought that each one is a rejection of a real inner enemy: arrogance, anger, heedlessness, desire, showing off, backbiting, and despair.
  • After Fajr, praise Allah, send salawat, and ask that your "provision" from this place be taqwa.

10 Dhu al-Hijjah: Mina and Eid

  • At Jamarat, say takbir with each pebble if possible. Make the stoning a spiritual rejection of Shaytan, not a moment of anger toward people.
  • At sacrifice, remember that Allah wants surrender, not meat. Ask: "What attachment must I sacrifice after Hajj?"
  • At halq/taqsir, ask Allah for light for every hair and for a real new beginning.
  • Begin the Eid takbir after prayers. In Mina, Makarem/Sistani mention takbir after fifteen prayers beginning Zuhr of Eid; elsewhere after ten prayers.

11-12 Dhu al-Hijjah: Mina

  • Remain in Mina with purpose. Treat the camp as a retreat: salah on time, Qur'an, salawat, helping others, and guarding the tongue.
  • If you can pray in Masjid al-Khaif, do so. The Sistani/al-Islam recommended acts page records special merit for prayer and dhikr there.
  • Before each Jamarah, name one sin or habit you want removed. Afterward, ask for steadfastness, not just relief.
  • Use waiting time for qadha prayers of life, Qur'an, short istighfar, and duas for people who asked.

Makkah and Masjid al-Haram

Move gently. In a crowd, not hurting people is itself part of the worship.

Ka'ba

  • On first sight, stop inwardly: praise Allah, send salawat, and ask for acceptance before asking for worldly needs.
  • Makarem mentions looking at the Ka'ba as a recommended act and narrates special mercy around the Ka'ba for those who look, pray, and perform tawaf.
  • Personal reflection: this is the qiblah of your body; ask Allah to make Him the qiblah of your choices.

Tawaf

  • Keep presence of heart. Makarem notes that talking, laughing, and eating do not invalidate tawaf, but it is better to avoid anything that reduces focus.
  • Use the duas.org Hajj and Umrah duas page for the transmitted tawaf duas.
  • Between Rukn Yamani and Hajar al-Aswad, the al-Islam/Sistani page includes the dua asking for good in this world and the next and protection from Hell.

Hajar al-Aswad

  • If possible without harm, touch or kiss it; if not, face it, gesture, and say takbir.
  • Do not push. Leaving a recommended act to protect another pilgrim is better than turning devotion into harm.
  • Spiritual intention: renew your covenant with Allah and the Ahl al-Bayt (a).

Rukn Yamani

  • When passing it in tawaf, make a quiet dua for goodness, forgiveness, and protection.
  • If you cannot reach it peacefully, keep walking and keep your heart present.
  • Reflection: ask Allah to make your "corner" in life stable: faith, family, livelihood, and death on wilayah.

Hijr Ismail

  • If crowd and rules allow, pray there and ask Allah through the patience of Ibrahim, Ismail, and Hajar (a).
  • Remember Hajar's trust: she acted, ran, searched, and still relied fully on Allah.
  • Make dua for children, parents, family healing, and provision that brings obedience.

Meezab e Rahma

  • The Sistani/al-Islam recommended acts page reports a dua of Imam Zayn al-Abidin (a) when looking toward the Meezab from the Hijr.
  • Ask for Paradise by Allah's mercy, safety from Hell, lawful sustenance, and protection from evil.
  • Reflection: stand under mercy without entitlement; ask to become merciful to others after Hajj.

Maqam Ibrahim

  • After obligatory tawaf, pray the two rak'ahs of tawaf near/behind Maqam Ibrahim according to your rulings and crowd conditions.
  • After prayer, thank Allah, send salawat, and ask Him to make your home, money, time, and body useful in obedience.
  • Reflection: Ibrahim (a) built while obeying. Ask for work that is accepted, not merely impressive.

Zamzam

  • Drink with intention. Makarem lists drinking Zamzam and reciting the dua for beneficial knowledge, wide sustenance, and cure from illness.
  • Use it as a moment to ask for pure knowledge, halal rizq, health for worship, and a heart that does not become hard.
  • Pouring some water on the head/body before sa'y is mentioned as recommended where practical.

Safa

  • Go with peace and dignity. Face the Ka'ba, praise Allah, remember His blessings, and say takbir, alhamdulillah, and la ilaha illa Allah.
  • Stay longer if you can without blocking others. The Sistani/al-Islam page records recommendation to prolong standing at Safa for increase in provision.
  • Reflection: begin your sa'y by admitting that effort and tawakkul belong together.

Marwa

  • Repeat the dhikr and duas of Safa. Sa'y should be with earnestness, weeping, and intense supplication where possible.
  • Ask for patience to complete what Allah starts in you; Marwa is a place of finishing, not quitting.
  • After sa'y and taqsir, make a sajdah of gratitude if possible and ask Allah not to let the journey become only memories.

Hajj Places

For the days when the body is tired and the heart needs direction.

Mina

  • Make Mina a camp of restraint: no arguing, backbiting, random sinful talk, or harshness.
  • Pray in Masjid al-Khaif if feasible. Keep the Eid takbir after prayers according to the recommended schedule.
  • Daily practice: one page Qur'an, one block of qadha salah, one list of people to pray for, and one act of service.

Arafat

  • This is the main day of asking. Do not spend the golden hours in chatter or phone scrolling.
  • Read Dua Arafah of Imam Husayn (a), then make your own duas plainly: faith, repentance, family, death with iman, and the Imam (a).
  • Keep a written "sins to leave" list: anger, lust, overeating, ingratitude, heedlessness, shirk in subtle forms, self-admiration, envy, laziness, backbiting, showing off, despair, and cruelty.

Mash'ar / Muzdalifah

  • Spend the night between awe and rest. You are between confession at Arafat and rejection of Shaytan at Mina.
  • Collect pebbles calmly and a few extra. Turn each pebble into an intention against a specific weakness.
  • After Fajr, praise Allah, thank Him for the chance to stand there, and ask Him to accept your excuse.

Jamarat

  • Have wudhu if possible, say takbir with each pebble, and avoid anger in the crowd.
  • Before throwing, say inwardly: "O Allah, I reject the Shaytan outside me and the Shaytan inside me."
  • Afterward, ask for a Hajj mabrur, accepted deeds, thanked effort, and forgiven sins.

Madinah

Visit with quietness, salawat, permission, and loyalty to the Prophet (s) and his family (a).

Masjid al-Nabawi

  • Sistani's recommended acts page says it is very strongly recommended for the pilgrim to return via Madinah and visit the Prophet (s), Sayyidah Fatimah (a), and the Imams of Baqi (a).
  • Enter with salawat, humility, and permission. Keep your voice low and your heart awake.
  • Read Ziyarat of the Prophet (s), then ask Allah to make you worthy of his intercession and sunnah.

Riyazul Jannah / Rawdah

  • If you are able to enter without harming anyone, pray two rak'ahs and make concise duas.
  • Focus on closeness to the Prophet (s): salawat, love of the Ahl al-Bayt (a), and a promise to improve your akhlaq.
  • If access is limited, do not let disappointment ruin adab. Send salawat from wherever you are in the masjid.

Sayyidah Fatimah (a)

  • Offer salams to her near the Prophet's mosque and in Baqi according to the ziyarat practice you follow.
  • Recite Tasbih al-Zahra (a), ask for modesty, courage, patience, and protection from hurting the Imam of your time.
  • Reflection: ask what kind of private worship and public loyalty your Hajj should produce.

Baqi

  • Adab al-Haramayn lists the Imams of Baqi (a): Imam Hasan, Imam Zayn al-Abidin, Imam Muhammad al-Baqir, and Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (a), and gives isti'dhan and ziyarat texts.
  • Stand with grief and dignity. Recite ziyarat, salawat, Qur'an, and Fatiha for the believers buried there.
  • Pray for the rebuilding of respect for the Ahl al-Bayt (a), for the dead, and for your own death to be on wilayah.

Ashab al-Suffa

  • Remember poverty, sincerity, learning, and service near the Prophet (s).
  • Ask Allah for freedom from greed, love of knowledge, humility with scholars, and the ability to serve without needing recognition.
  • Short practice: salawat, Surah al-Fatihah, and a promise to support seekers of knowledge and needy believers.

Uhud and Hamza (a)

  • Send salams to Sayyid al-Shuhada Hamza (a) and the martyrs of Uhud.
  • Recite Fatiha, salawat, and ask Allah for courage without recklessness, loyalty without ego, and steadfastness after mistakes.
  • Reflection: Uhud teaches that love needs obedience, discipline, and staying at the post assigned by Allah.

Quba and Qiblatayn

  • At Quba, pray if possible and ask for a mosque-like heart: clean, simple, and built on taqwa.
  • At Qiblatayn, remember that a believer turns when Allah commands. Ask for quick obedience when truth becomes clear.
  • Keep visits calm and purposeful; do not let photos replace ziyarat.

When Tired or Overwhelmed

A small plan is better than losing the whole hour.

Five-Minute Spiritual Reset

  • 10 salawat with attention.
  • One sincere istighfar for a named sin.
  • Surah al-Fatihah for your parents, teachers, marhumeen, and believers.
  • One dua for Imam al-Mahdi (a) and the oppressed.
  • One qadha salah of your life when you have enough time and focus.
